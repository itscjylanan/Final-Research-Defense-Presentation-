Introduction

A beautiful and welcoming environment for students can cultivate a sense of safety and belonging, encouraging them to look forward to attending each day. This positive atmosphere can boost their self-esteem, elevate their expectations, and strengthen their connections with others, ultimately enhancing their focus, engagement, and overall enjoyment in school (Alomes, 2023).


The quality of physical infrastructure in schools has been shown to impact student performance directly. Studies conducted in the United States show that student accomplishment levels are greater in schools with up-to-date, well-maintained infrastructure (Baker et al., 2919). Moreover, a suitable learning environment is said to require optimal temperature management, adequate ventilation, and good lighting. According to the U.S. Department of Education (2019), inadequate infrastructure, such as crowded classrooms and dilapidated buildings, has a detrimental effect on students' cognitive and physical health, which in turn affects their capacity to concentrate and study.

Review of Related Literature

This chapter contains a literature review of relevant studies and initiatives based on the nature of strategic planning, designing, and managing a facility's space, which entails designing flexible spaces, incorporating technology, and the various themes to categorize the development to address each step needed to improve the school development.
